True to our open-source philosophy, all deliverables will be public from day one. We will not maintain any private repositories under our GitHub organization, ensuring full transparency and attribution. Every contributor will receive due credit, and we collectively aim to produce work of the highest quality—worthy of publication at top-tier AI conferences.

You are also more than welcome to launch your own startups based on what we build here. In fact, we see immense potential for numerous unicorns to emerge from AI4Marketing research effort. AI-native startups, even within the same field, are not competitors with each other—they are allies challenging the old giants still clinging to traditional ways of doing things.

Research Pondering

Our research is motivated by a breakdown of the roles within a typical social marketing team in traditional companies. This decomposition leads us to pursue two parallel research directions: ViralBench and POGA (Prompt Optimization for Generative AI).

Social marketing today requires a nuanced understanding of platform algorithms, emerging trends, brand playbooks, cultural codes, and strategic timing. Once these insights are gathered, they are often handed off to creative teams responsible for producing short-form, high-impact videos for platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube.

We see a natural division here:

The first arm, focused on insight generation and strategic reasoning, is well-suited for augmentation by agentic AI systems.

The second arm, centered on content creation, aligns with the capabilities of generative video models.

However, two urgent research gaps remain:

Evaluation Gap for Social Marketing Agents → ViralBench

There is currently no standardized or meaningful way to evaluate whether AI agents actually "understand" social marketing or can reason about what makes content go viral.

Steerability Gap for Creative Tools → POGA

Many creators find today's generative video models difficult to control and unreliable in execution.

POGA (Prompt Optimization for Generative AI) aims to build systems that help creators better steer generative models toward desired outcomes—bridging the gap between creative intent and generative output.

We believe these are some of the most urgent and impactful research problems in the emerging intersection of AI, creativity, and social media.

Check out our individual GitHub repositories for more technical details on both ViralBench and POGA.
